This tool was built as a Group 4 assignment project for the Department of Cyber Security, Federal University Dutse (FUD), 200 Level. It is a free, browser-based security toolkit that helps everyday users build stronger passwords and stay safe online.

No account is needed. No data is stored. Everything runs privately inside your browser.

Type any password into the checker box. The tool instantly analyses it against 4 requirements:

  • At least 8 characters long
  • Contains at least one number (0‚Äì9)
  • Contains at least one symbol (!@#$%^&*)
  • Contains both uppercase and lowercase letters

Each requirement met adds to the score. The meter fills from Weak ‚Üí Medium ‚Üí Strong ‚Üí Unbreakable.

The crack time shows how long it would take a powerful computer making 100 billion guesses per second to brute-force your password.

It calculates this by looking at your password's character set size and length ‚Äî the longer and more varied the password, the longer it takes to crack.

After you stop typing, the tool checks your password against a database of billions of leaked passwords from real data breaches.

Your privacy is protected ‚Äî the full password is never sent. Instead, only the first 5 characters of a scrambled hash are sent. This is called the k-anonymity model.

If your password is found, stop using it immediately on all your accounts.

Can't think of a strong password? Let the generator create one for you:

  • Adjust the word count slider (2‚Äì6 words)
  • Toggle Numbers and Symbols on or off
  • Click Generate to create a new passphrase
  • Click Copy to copy it to your clipboard

Passphrases like Quantum-Shield-42! are much stronger than single-word passwords.

We never store, log, or transmit your full password. All strength checks happen locally inside your browser. The breach check only sends a partial hash ‚Äî your actual password never leaves your device.

This tool is safe to use on any device. You do not need to create an account or provide any personal information.

Avoid these common mistakes that make passwords easy to crack:

  • Using your name, birthday, or phone number
  • Simple substitutions like P@ssw0rd ‚Äî still weak!
  • Reusing the same password on multiple sites
  • Using only dictionary words alone
  • Passwords shorter than 12 characters
  • Sharing passwords via WhatsApp or email

Understanding attack methods helps you defend against them:

  • Brute Force ‚Äî trying every possible combination until one works
  • Dictionary Attack ‚Äî using lists of common words and passwords
  • Credential Stuffing ‚Äî using leaked username/password pairs from other breaches
  • Phishing ‚Äî tricking you into typing your password on a fake site

A long, unique, symbol-rich password defeats brute force and dictionary attacks completely.

  • Use a passphrase instead of a single word ‚Äî longer is stronger
  • Enable 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication) on every account you can
  • Use a password manager app to store unique passwords safely
  • Never enter passwords on public Wi-Fi without a VPN
  • Change your most important passwords every 3‚Äì6 months
